All that glisters is not gold

(Open) data puts us at risk!



5 minute assignment: Think about risks of open data for (1) individuals, and (2) companies.

(Open) data risks

For individuals: identity theft, fraud, stalking, discrimination, blackmail, deepfakes

For companies: leakage of trade secrets, customer targeting by competitors, copyright or IP issues, reputational damage

For the general public: misinformation, surveillance, unequal power, loss of trust, drowning in low-quality data, and environmental costs of storing and processing massive datasets

Some known data breaches

  • Netflix prize data

  • Strava runs of military personnel

  • And of course all kinds of hacks

Synthetic data

Synthetic data are data that are generated from a model or algorithm, with the aim of mimicking (some aspects of) the observed data.1

  1. As opposed to real world, collected data.

Using synthetic data

We want to do the same things with synthetic data that we could do with the real data, without the privacy risks related to the real data.
